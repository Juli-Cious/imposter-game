## **The Narrative Arc (3 distinct phases)**

### **Phase 1: The "Boring Validator" (The Failure)**
*   **What we built:** Initially, we just built a tool that checked if code was correct.
*   **The Feedback:** Students (Age 12) said, *"I don't know why my code failed, and I don't care. This feels like homework."*
*   **The Realization:** We were teaching **syntax** (what to write) but missing **engagement** (why it matters).
*   **The Pivot:** We realized we needed *tension* and *competition*.

### **Phase 2: The "Imposter" Insight (The Mechanic)**
*   **What we changed:** We introduced the "Sabotage" mechanic. One player breaks the code; the other fixes it.
*   **The Feedback:** *"The sabotage is fun, but the levels are all the same. Once I memorize the answer, it's too easy."*
*   **The Solution:** We integrated **Gemini Pro (The Chaos Engine)**. Instead of 10 hard-coded levels, we now had **infinite unique levels**.
*   **The Result:** Replayability skyrocketed. Students played for 45 minutes straight instead of 5.

### **Phase 3: Making it "Green" (The Impact)**
*   **What we saw:** Students were fixing the code, but they didn't care about *efficiency*. They just wanted it to run.
*   **The Feedback:** *"Why does O(nÂ²) matter? It works, doesn't it?"* The environmental impact was too abstract.
*   **The Fix:** We replaced "Time Complexity" with **"Real World Energy"**.
